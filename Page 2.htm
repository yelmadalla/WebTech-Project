HEC Paris is an extremely prestigious business school in France and one of the top 10 business schools in Europe. In the most recent Financial Times ranking, HEC has surpassed others as the #1 Business School in Europe. It's a graduate school that offers a Masters in Business.

Where should I live?

The best option for students studying at HEC would be to live at ECLA. It's a residence in Massy Palaiseau, about a 45 minute train ride, or 15 minute car ride from HEC. The map below shows the exact location of ECLA, in Massy-Palaiseau, as compared to the HEC Campus in Jouy-en-Josas.

Where are the ammenities around me?

Massy Palaiseau is a great town to live in. It's fairly new and has lots of supermarkets, cafes, movie theatres, and more entertainment nearby. Everything is walking distance which is optimal when not having a dining hall and to hang out with friends from abroad.

What courses should I take?

Babson allows us to take many valuable courses at HEC, whilst the program is pass/fail the classes HEC offers are extremely interesting and are different than ones at Babson College. If you're interested in anything luxury or fashion related, then HEC has many business classes to offer in those areas that count for Babson credits too.

How tough are the courses?

With regards to HEC courses they are very similar to Babson courses in the way they are taught and evaluated. The class sizes are fairly small, reaching a maximum of 40 students for the foundation courses, thus participation and attendance is evaluated. Additionally, there are a lot of group projects as compared to individual work just like at Babson.
